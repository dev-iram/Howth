PLACES OF INTEREST AROUND..


BAILY LIGHTHOUSE

The Baily Lighthouse is a lighthouse on the southeastern part of Howth Head in Dublin, Ireland. It is maintained by the Commissioners of Irish Lights.

BRASS MONKEY

Quaint, harbourside eatery and bar, serving European tapas and fresh-caught fish and meat dishes.

HOWTH VILLAGE

Howth is an Irish village on the Howth Peninsula, east of central Dublin.

HOWTH CASTLE

Turn the pages of history, step by step, exploring the upstairs and downstairs of Howth Castle, the intimate, private and warm hearth of history, legend and mythology.

HOWTH CLIFF PATH

The Howth Cliff Path Loop Walk is a moderate 6 to 10km (2 to 3 hrs) loop walk taking in some easy road walking, rugged but superb cliff walking and great scenery.

HOWTH CLIFF WALK

Coastal walk taken through the Green Route with amazing coastal views. Cannot be missed if visiting Howth.

THE SUMMIT

Howth Summit (171m) has excellent views across Dublin Bay right down to County Wicklow..

HOWTH MARKET

Outdoor weekend market featuring locally made foods, organic produce, artisanal goods & souvenirs.

HOWTH RAILWAY STATION

Howth DART station, is a railway station in Fingal, Ireland that serves Howth village and one side of Howth Head.

IRELAND'S EYE

Ireland's Eye is a small uninhabited island off the coast of County Dublin, Ireland, situated directly north of Howth Harbour. The island is easily reached by regular tourist boats.

MARTELLO TOWER

Stalwart tower & historic site housing a museum of vintage collectible radios & memorabilia.

THE OAR HOUSE

Old stone fisherman's cottage venue for simply prepared fresh fish dishes on a village pierside.

TRANSPORT MUSEUM HOWTH

The museum is located in the Heritage Depot, Howth Demesne, Howth, Ireland. 60 vehicles are currently in Howth on display. The oldest items date from second half of the 19th century, the newest 1984.

HOWTH LIGHTHOUSE

Howth Harbour Lighthouse, built in 1817, is located at the end of Howth Harbour.

About Howth

Howth is a picturesque village clustered around and above a busy sailing and fishing port on the north coast of the rugged peninsula, which forms the northern side of Dublin Bay on Ireland's East Coast.
